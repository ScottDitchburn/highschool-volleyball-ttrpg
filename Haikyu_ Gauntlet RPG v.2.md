# Haikyu: Gauntlet RPG v.2

## Physical Attributes:

1) For each physical attribute you roll 3d10, or use the command ![][image1] three times. Then add all the rolls.  
   1) Repeat this step again, so at the end you have two numbers that are between 3-30.  
2) Then assign each number to a Physical Attribute; either Height or Vertical Jump.  
   1) See the [Physical Attributes Table](#bookmark=id.5qfl608wgs64) for respected value to centimeter conversions.  
3) In game physical characteristics can be calculated from your Physical Attributes. To do so use the below equations:

### Standing Reach Equation:

1.3 \* Height

### Spiking Reach Equation:

1.3 \* Height \+ Vertical Jump 

### Blocking Reach Equation:

1.3 \* Height \+ 0.85\* Vertical Jump 

Skill Stats:

1) For each Skill Stat you roll 4d4, or use the command ![][image2] four times. Then average all rolls.  
   1) Repeat this step nine more times, so at the end you have 10 numbers that are between 1-4.  
2) Then assign each number to a Skill Stat.  
   1) See the table below for all ten [Skill Stats](#bookmark=id.w80dq5y64651).

| Skill Stat Table |  |
| ----- | :---- |
| Skill Stat | Description |
| Spike | Your technical ability to spike the ball. (Bokuto’s sharp line spike.) |
| Serve | The toughness of your serve and the ability to place it. (Atsumu’s float serve drifting at the least moment.) |
| Pass | Your ability to control the ball off a serve. (Yaku’s unparalleled and accurate bump to start a rally.) |
| Dig | The ability to keep balls up from spikes or other means. (Nishinoya diving to keep the ball up off a block.) |
| Set | Your ability to set up your teammates to allow them to score. (Kageyama’s ability to set from anywhere on the court.) |
| Block | The ability to stop or hinder any spiker attempting to score. (Tsukishima dropping his hands perfectly into the seam.) |
| Speed | Your sheer movement to get from place to place. (Hinata running from across the court in just a blink.) |
| Power | The sheer ability to tear through any block. (Ushijima spiking through a well formed triple block.) |
| IQ | To know every option available and choose the best one. (Hoshiumi seeing the block press late, and tooling off it.) |
| Stamina | Your ability to not waiver as the set and match presses onward. (Asashi, pushing forward in his approach, form perfect through the air.) |

Schooling Year:

1) Roll 1d3 to determine your Schooling Year. Then consult the table below for potential bonuses to your Ability Points (As described next section).

| School Year Table |  |
| ----- | :---- |
| Roll 1d3: | Bonuses: |
| 1st Year | No Bonuses. |
| 2nd Year | An additional 3+2d4 AP |
| 3rd Year | An additional 6+4d4 AP |

2) After a player has played in both the Summer Interhigh and Spring Interhigh tournament, they move up a year. When they do so, instead of consulting the table, they instead gain additional AP described in the equation below.

### AP Gain per Year:

AP Gain=3+2\*\# Teams Played

Furthermore, you grow during the course of the off season. You Height is increased at the end of	each year. To calculate the increase in height, roll 1d20 and multiply it by 0.1 cm.

1) If a 3rd year were to graduate, that player may then make another character.

3) Lastly a player may have previous experience that gives them the slightest edge over their teammates of the same school year. Roll 2d8 and consult the table below to determine your experience.

Abilities (WIP):

1) A character has 10 Ability Points they may spend after they allocate their Physical Attributes and their Skill Stats.   
   1) Some Abilities require a prerequisite to purchase. You must meet the prerequisite in order to purchase the ability. (Example, an ability with a Prerequisite of “Dig 2+ means you must have a Dig Stat equal to 2 or greater.)  
      1) Some Prerequisites have a number in parentheses such as “(4)”. Abilities with these numbers can be taken at max that number of times.  
   2) Other abilities may be tiered. You may purchase this ability at its base cost for its base effect. Every tier thereafter, you must pay an additional cost to improve the ability or unlock the additional effect. If you wish to go from Tier I to Tier IV, you must pay the Additional Costs for Tiers II, III, & IV.
